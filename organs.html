Major Organs of the Respiratory System

The respiratory system is made up of organs that work together to help us breathe. Each organ has a specific role in the process of taking in oxygen and removing carbon dioxide from the body.

Nose and Nasal Cavity

Definition: The nose is the external part of the respiratory system and the nasal cavity is the internal space behind it.

Function: These structures serve as the primary entrance for outside air into the respiratory system. They warm, filter, and moisten the air before it travels to the lungs.

Learn More

Trachea (Windpipe)

Definition: The trachea is a tube-like structure that connects the larynx to the bronchi.

Function: It allows air to pass from the throat to the lungs. Its rigid structure, supported by cartilage rings, prevents collapse during breathing.

Learn More

Bronchi and Bronchioles

Definition: The bronchi are the two large branches of the trachea that enter the lungs and further divide into smaller tubes called bronchioles.

Function: They distribute air throughout the lungs and control the flow of air to the alveoli where gas exchange occurs.

Learn More

Lungs

Definition: The lungs are two large, spongy organs located in the chest cavity.

Function: They house millions of alveoli that absorb oxygen from inhaled air and release carbon dioxide during exhalation.

Learn More

Diaphragm

Definition: The diaphragm is a large, dome-shaped muscle located below the lungs.

Function: It contracts and flattens when you inhale, creating a vacuum that pulls air into the lungs. It relaxes when you exhale, pushing air out.
