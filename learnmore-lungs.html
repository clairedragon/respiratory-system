Lungs

The lungs are two large, spongy organs located in the chest cavity, protected by the rib cage. The right lung is divided into three lobes while the left lung has two lobes to accommodate space for the heart.

Each lung contains millions of tiny air sacs called alveoli, which provide a large surface area for gas exchange. Oxygen from inhaled air diffuses through the thin walls of the alveoli into the surrounding capillaries, entering the bloodstream.

At the same time, carbon dioxide, a waste product of metabolism, diffuses from the blood into the alveoli to be exhaled out of the body.

The lungs also contain elastic fibers that allow them to expand and recoil during breathing. The pleura, a double-layered membrane surrounding the lungs, produces fluid to reduce friction during lung movements.

In addition to breathing, the lungs play roles in regulating blood pH and serving as filters to trap small blood clots and air bubbles in the bloodstream.
