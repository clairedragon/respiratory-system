Nose and Nasal Cavity

The nose and nasal cavity are the first parts of the respiratory tract. They provide the main entrance for air entering the respiratory system.

The nasal cavity is lined with mucous membranes and tiny hair-like structures called cilia. These help trap dust, pollutants, and microorganisms, preventing them from reaching the lungs.

As air passes through the nasal cavity, it is warmed and humidified, which protects the delicate tissues in the lower respiratory tract. The nose also contains olfactory receptors, allowing us to detect smells.

The structure and function of the nose are essential for effective breathing and protecting the body from harmful substances. Additionally, the nasal cavity assists in filtering and cleaning inhaled air before it reaches the lungs.

Proper functioning of the nose and nasal cavity helps maintain respiratory health and prevents infections by acting as the body’s first line of defense.
