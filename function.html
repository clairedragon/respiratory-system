Common Respiratory Conditions

Asthma

Definition: Asthma is a chronic disease that inflames and narrows the airways, causing difficulty in breathing.

Symptoms: Wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, and coughing.

Causes: Triggered by allergens, exercise, cold air, or respiratory infections.

Treatment: Inhalers, avoiding triggers, and medications prescribed by doctors.

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

Definition: COPD is a group of lung diseases including emphysema and chronic bronchitis that block airflow and make breathing difficult.

Symptoms: Persistent cough, mucus production, shortness of breath, and frequent respiratory infections.

Causes: Mainly caused by long-term exposure to irritants like cigarette smoke.

Treatment: Smoking cessation, medications, pulmonary rehabilitation, and oxygen therapy.

Pneumonia

Definition: Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs which may fill with fluid or pus.

Symptoms: Fever, chills, cough with phlegm, chest pain, and difficulty breathing.

Causes: Bacterial, viral, or fungal infections.

Treatment: Antibiotics for bacterial pneumonia, rest, fluids, and sometimes hospitalization.

Bronchitis

Definition: Bronchitis is inflammation of the bronchial tubes, leading to coughing and mucus production.

Symptoms: Persistent cough, wheezing, fatigue, and slight fever.

Causes: Usually caused by viral infections or exposure to tobacco smoke and pollutants.

Treatment: Rest, fluids, cough suppressants, and avoiding irritants.

Tuberculosis (TB)

Definition: TB is a contagious bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs but can spread to other organs.

Symptoms: Chronic cough, weight loss, fever, night sweats, and fatigue.

Causes: Caused by the bacteria Mycobacterium tuberculosis, spread through airborne droplets.

Treatment: Long-term antibiotics prescribed by healthcare professionals.
